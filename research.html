Research Program

Research

RNA transcripts are far more than static snapshots as seen in single-cell sequencing datasets. They are dynamic molecules, constantly being transcribed, transported, translated and degraded with precise timing. These spatiotemporal RNA dynamics and their localized translation represent a powerful but underexplored layer of regulation in development, stem cell function, and aging.

Yet, existing spatial transcriptomics only capture the static "snapshots" of RNA, which makes it difficult to probe these dynamics processes in heterogeneous cell populations. To address this gap, Rena has developed imaging-based in situ profiling tools that spatially resolve post-transcriptional RNA dynamics at transcriptome scale in single cells. This work produced two technologies: (1) TEMPOmap, which measures where and when RNAs are newly transcribed, transported and degraded, and (2) RIBOmap, which measures where translation occurs at genomic level. These technologies allowed us, for the first time, to holistically profile single-molecule RNA transcripts at the levels of spatial localization, temporal dynamics and translation within single cells at the genomic level.

Rena's research program focuses on developing and utilizing the next-generation spatial and temporal RNA sequencing technologies to visualize the life cycle of RNA directly in intact cells and tissues. By doing so, the group aims to uncover when, where, and how RNA processes are regulated in space and time, and how these dynamics shape cell fate and tissue organization in health and disease.
